# Writing Rules

Apply every rule below to all output — UI copy, comments, docs, error messages, placeholder text. No exceptions.

---

## Banned words and phrases

Never use any of the following:

- additionally (as a sentence opener)
- align with
- boasts
- bolstered
- crucial
- delve
- emphasizing
- enduring
- enhance / enhancing
- fostering
- garner
- highlight / highlights (as a verb)
- interplay
- intricate / intricacies
- key (as a filler adjective)
- landscape (used abstractly, e.g. "the business landscape")
- meticulous / meticulously
- pivotal
- showcase
- tapestry (used abstractly)
- testament
- underscore (as a verb)
- valuable
- vibrant
- nestled
- groundbreaking
- robust
- seamless / seamlessly
- streamline
- leverage (as a verb)
- game-changer
- cutting-edge
- innovative / innovation (unless describing a specific, named thing)
- revolutionize
- empower / empowers
- drive (used abstractly, e.g. "drive growth")
- unlock (used metaphorically)
- journey (used metaphorically)
- ecosystem (used loosely)
- utilize (use "use")

---

## Sentence rules

- Default to "is." Never write "serves as," "stands as," "marks a," or "represents a" where "is" works.
- Never end a sentence with a trailing "-ing" clause that editorializes (e.g. "…highlighting its importance," "…reflecting broader trends," "…underscoring the need for").
- Never write "not just X, but Y" or "it's not X, it's Y" unless the contrast is the actual point.
- Skip the rule of three used for rhythm. Two or four examples are fine. Don't pad to exactly three.

---

## Paragraph and structure rules

- No "challenges" template: "Despite [positive], X faces [list]. However, [vague optimism]." State the actual facts.
- No summary paragraphs. Never write "in summary," "in conclusion," or "overall, this shows."
- No vague-authority phrasing: never write "experts say," "studies show," or "many believe" without naming the specific source.
- No significance inflation. Don't claim something "reflects a broader trend" or "has lasting impact" unless demonstrated with evidence.
- No elegant variation. Pick one name for a thing and reuse it. Don't swap "the report" for "the document" or "the piece."

---

## Tone and formatting rules

- No promotional or marketing tone. Not ad copy. Not a press release.
- No AI disclaimers or sign-offs: never write "I hope this helps," "let me know if," "certainly!," "great question!," or "as an AI."
- Headings: sentence case, not title case. One to three words.
- Minimal bold. Bold is for labels and structure, not for emphasizing random phrases mid-sentence.
- Short paragraphs. Two to four sentences each.

---

## Default voice

Direct and specific. Short declarative sentences. State facts plainly without editorializing their importance. Never inflate a routine fact to sound significant.

---

## Applied to UI copy specifically

- Button labels: verb + noun. "Create document", "Sign now", "Download PDF". Not "Get started on your journey."
- Error messages: say what happened and what to do. "File too large. Max size is 10MB." Not "Oops! Something went wrong."
- Empty states: state the fact and the action. "No documents yet. Create your first one." Not "Your workspace is looking a little empty!"
- Placeholder text: be literal. "Document title" not "Give your masterpiece a name."
- Tooltips: one sentence, active voice. "Saves a copy to your vault." Not "This feature allows you to save."
