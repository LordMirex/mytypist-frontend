Why this mark

Abstract first, literal on a second look. Three of the four facets are pure geometry. The fourth — top, lightest — is a small folded-corner page. Same trick Notion uses on the "N": you read the whole thing as a clean abstract mark, and once you notice the page, you can't un-see it.

The geometry still means something. Four facets, each a step lighter clockwise, turning into one motion — your own homepage already says it: "one structured pipeline," not four disconnected tools.

Tested at the sizes it'll actually ship at. The page-fold is a bonus you notice at logo size; at 16px favicon scale it simplifies back down to a clean four-point mark, never a smudge.

The hidden detail

Top-left facet only: a literal dog-eared page corner, same color family as the rest. Zoom in on any export and it's unmistakable — zoom out and it's just one bright facet in the spin.
